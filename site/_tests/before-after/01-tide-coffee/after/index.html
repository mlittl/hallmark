No. 01 · April 2026 · Single-Origin Subscription

Fresh beans, once a month, from one farm.

Tide is a coffee subscription built around a single principle: the farm matters. Every month, a different origin. Every bag, roasted and shipped within 48 hours.

Good coffee begins before the roast — in soil, altitude, varietal, and weather. These are things that don't survive blending, but survive very well inside a single bag from a single farm.

We started Tide because we were tired of coffee that tasted like "coffee." The category-neutral roast. The hedge-everything blend. The bag that could have come from anywhere. Single-origin is a refusal of that logic — a commitment to naming the place, the farmer, the season.

The tide in the name is not metaphor. It is rhythm. Every month something new comes to shore. You live with it for four weeks, then the next origin arrives. Over a year you taste twelve countries — twelve different expressions of what coffee becomes when it isn't averaged into a blend.

This month · April 2026

The origin.

This month we are shipping from the Konga Cooperative in the Yirgacheffe zone of southern Ethiopia — one of the oldest coffee-growing regions in the world, and likely where Coffea arabica first grew wild before anyone thought to cultivate it.

Konga Cooperative
Yirgacheffe, Ethiopia

Natural process · Heirloom varieties · Altitude 1,800–2,200 m

"Jasmine, stone fruit, dark chocolate — long finish."

The Konga farmers use a natural process: the cherries dry whole on raised beds in the highland sun. It is older than wet-processing by centuries, and it produces a cup that tastes, in the best possible way, like the fruit it came from.

How it works.

Once a month, a 250 g bag of freshly-roasted single-origin beans arrives at your door. We select the origin; you select the grind — whole bean, filter, or espresso. No two consecutive months are the same country.
