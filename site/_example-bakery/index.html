01  ⁄  What we bake

A short list, done properly.

  • 01 Country Stone-milled wheat, a little rye, a long cold ferment. Our everyday loaf. Daily
  • 02 Seeded Pumpkin, sesame, poppy. The country base with a denser crumb. Daily
  • 03 Miche A broad round, 1.4 kg. Keeps four days on the counter, longer under cloth. Fri & Sat
  • 04 Rye Seventy percent rye, caraway, a little molasses. Dense, dark, patient. Thursdays

We bake in small numbers because we’d rather bake four loaves well than forty in a hurry. If a loaf is sold out by ten, that’s how it goes.

02  ⁄  Why

“Good bread is mostly time — a long fermentation, a patient oven, and flour that wasn’t rushed.” — from the opening letter

03  ⁄  Flour & farmers

Milled in Paxton, grown twenty-three miles away.

Our flour is stone-milled at the Paxton mill, north of town. The wheat comes from two farms — Linnea Berg’s in the valley and the Holdfast Co-op on the ridge. We work with what they grow, when they grow it, which means the flour shifts a little with the seasons. We think that’s a feature.
