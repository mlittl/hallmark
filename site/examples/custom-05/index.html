07:12 Shorelines

Brindle Sound, low water

54.142°N  3.018°W · 14:11

Forty minutes before the lowest tide of the year. Shingle drains between waves; the whole beach ticks as the water lets go of it. A curlew, twice, far off. Recorded prone, mics a hand's width from the wet stone.

15:48 Weather

A front comes in over the flats

54.097°N  3.106°W · 06:30

No shelter for two miles, so the wind has nothing to hold and nothing to break it. It arrives as pressure first, then as a long unbroken sheet of rain on standing water. The loudest thing here is also the emptiest.

24:03 Rooms & machines

The pump house, after hours

53.998°N  2.940°W · 23:40

A brick room the size of a chapel, one motor turning over once a minute to keep the level. Between cycles the room rings — a low, patient note you feel before you hear. I left the recorder running and stood very still until it forgot me.

33:20 Method

How the quiet gets kept

Nothing here is sweetened. The work is choosing where to stand, how long to wait, and what to leave in.

  • 01

    Arrive early enough to be boring. Wildlife and weather both ignore a person who has stopped being interesting, which is usually about twenty minutes.

  • 02

    The room is an instrument. A pump house, a stairwell, a culvert — each has a note it wants to hold. The recording is mostly about finding it.

  • 03

    Keep the long takes. The minute before something happens is the recording; the event is just punctuation.
